TEAM MANAGEMENT
  • "Manage staff- hiring & firing "

    I've hired, managed and replaced vendors, employees and even customers across a range of industries and situations.

    When terminating working relationships, I have always dealt with these matters personally, and never emotionally. I always try to handle such unfortunate occurrences tactfully, professionally and firmly.

    I believe that hiring the best people is critical to success in any business.

    Taking care of your team is critical to your team taking care of you.

    Replacing employees that are not right for the role or the company is also critical to team success and morale.

  • "Create job descriptions"

    Creating a good job description is critical to finding the right person for a job, and I consider myself quite good at this. The right job description can save a lot of time and frustration.

    My process starts with determining the skillsets, levels of experience and personality fit needed, then writing the description with the correct amount of detail and in the correct voice.

  • "Define client assignments and manage staff workload"

    Matching the right account manager with the right client is critical to creating a happy client. This can be tricky, and must be handled delicately, and I would take a great deal of care in this task.

    My process would be to find a balance between relationship fit and workload responsibilities - sometimes you can't put your first choice of account manager with a client, and pulling someone from an existing job and stable customer relationship can be very damaging to all involved.

  • "Ensure all departments efficiently work together toward established goals"

    Much like the ringmaster of a circus, this is something I excel at and enjoy - maintaining relationships, recognizing priorities, understanding timing issues and task difficulties, communicating, removing roadblocks and directing traffic.

    I have done this in a number of roles over the years, dealing with a wide range of departments and personalities.

  • "Maintain responsibility for employee performance and dept work quality"

    A leader is ultimately responsible for their team's performance - particularly when that leader is responsible for creating and directing the team.

    I don't believe in passing the buck or throwing people under the bus to save one's own skin. If I make a mistake, I own it.

    I am the kind of leader that is very loyal to my team, and I always do everything in my power to ensure their success, even if that means pitching-in and helping with tasks that are not strictly in my job description.

  • "Serve as the departments final arbiter on project and process questions"

    I am used to making big decisions about projects and processes, but I also believe strongly in listening to all input and advice in order to make the most informed decision possible.

    In the end though, I will make the decision I feel is best for the team, even if it's not the most popular decision.

  • "Determine needs and create and manage dept processes and tools"

    This has a lot to do with maintaining open communication with the team.

    A good leader is always listening.

    If there are consistent pain points arising, it's always a good idea to explore options for improvement. Sometimes there may be no immediate or practical solution, but I believe strongly in finding better ways to do things.

    It's also wise to talk and listen to the people on the front lines and ask for their input, because they usually have relevant information and insights, and might have the best solutions.

  • "Train and onboard new and existing employees"

    Bringing on a new team member without thoroughly explaining processes and expectations is a waste of everyone's time.

    Spending the time necessary to get a new person up-to-speed is time and money well-spent in the long run.

    I also believe in creating a safe environment where asking questions is encouraged across the team, and this helps the onboarding process tremendously.

  • "Encourage and deliver innovation to the dept and motivate employee performance"

    I seek constant improvement in everything I do, and I would encourage this trait in my team.

    I think some sort of incentive program would be a good way to encourage process and performance improvement, as would maintaining an atmosphere of openness and an ear to the development of new technologies and processes.

  • "Conduct team and dept planning and goal setting"

    I would like to see this done largely as a team, with me as the facilitator.

    Letting a team create their own goals and plan their own processes is a great way to encourage ownership and accountability.

    I also like to keep communication going continuously so that there are no surprises when it comes to goal achievement, and if process changes need to be made they can be made as soon as possible.

    Also see this example:
    * Team Goals Planning Spreadsheet

  • "Set tone and culture for the team"

    I would create a tone and culture of openness, safety (to question or make suggestions), cooperation (if it makes sense for you to bend your job description and pitch-in to help a teammate, do it!), life-balance and pride in accomplishment of the team.

    I don't take myself too seriously, and I'd like to see an environment where the team works hard and well, but at the same time are able to have fun, enjoy their jobs and have time to enjoy their lives outside of work as well.

I believe that if you take care of your team, they will take care of you.

ADMINISTRATIVE RESPONSIBILITIES
  • "Develop and oversee brief and bid development"

    I have written and received hundreds of briefs, and having been on both sides really helps my ability to do this well.

    As a business owner, I've bid and negotiated for dozens of jobs, and though I would need to learn about the W21 products, costings and bidding processes, I am confident in my ability to pick up and execute this responsibility.

  • "Ensure dept consistency, efficiency and effectiveness, including streamlining communications with Alabama operations"

    I would first be sure I understand the processes and expectations, then I would ask a lot of questions of everyone involved, including what is being done well and what could use improvement.

    I'd then distill all of this into a set of processes and policies, then I would educate all parties and ensure that everyone involved is on the same page.

    Then, with the advice and involvement of all parties (and an understanding of their needs and abilities), I would develop a communication protocol and schedule that would keep everyone connected and included.

  • "Contribute to client discovery and strategy processes"

    This is an area I really pride myself on and enjoy.

    Client discovery is so very important and difficult, and I have learned over years and years and dozens and dozens of clients that asking the right questions and really understanding their needs is absolutely critical to this process.

    I also believe in keeping the client involved in the strategy process as much as possible/sensible, so that there are no surprises and everyone saves time and energy.

  • "Scope & cost projects"

    I would obviously need to learn a lot more about the W21 products, costs and pricing policies, but I am comfortable with putting-together project proposals and bids, working out break-even and desired profit margins and calculating pricing.

  • "On a weekly basis, review all transactions in appropriate projects in the project management system to ensure that all labor and expenses are put toward accurate projects"

    This is a process that is critical to keeping on top of EVERYTHING, and could determine important adjustment to timelines, team resources, budgets or scope - the determinations of which would also need to be communicated to the appropriate teammates and/or clients as necessary.

  • "Review billing and invoicing on a monthly basis to ensure that all client billing is accurate and budgets are reconciled"

    This is basic housekeeping, and as a previous small business owner, something I am used to tracking and reviewing constantly.

    It's also just good customer service, and a critical measure of vendor performance.

  • "Approve timesheets for team members"

    This is another element of tracking project costs, and needs to be watched with as much care as any other cost element.

    It can also tell you if a project needs more (or less) team resources.

  • "Source, oversee and manage outsource agreements/decisions"

    Outsourced vendors are just as important as internal team members, and these partnerships must be fostered and monitored with care.

    Vendors should be made aware of our needs and expectations just as we must be aware of theirs.

    Vendors that are not performing acceptably must be replaced, just as with an internal team member.

I believe in the "servant leader" style of management.

  • "Execute for multi-channel strategies including print, in-store and events"
