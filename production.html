MANUFACTURING
  • "Familiarization with manufacturing technologies as they relate to exhibit building and construction for events and facilities in order to provide support to staff"

    I'm used to being on the client-side of this, but I've worked with a LOT of manufacturers of a huge range different products - from signage to print, merchandise, multi-page documents, banners, labels, clothing and more.

    Communication is key here:
    - Production needs to understand the customer's needs and their requirements, expectations and limitations.
    - The client needs to understand the manufacturing deadlines, art requirements and be clear about their needs (including budgets).

    Creating the correct product is key, which requires a great deal of communication, prototyping, discussion and logistic planning before production begins.

  • "In partnership with other event and production staff, specify the equipment utilized to produce work, including the use of a CNC machine"

    This is something I would need to learn, but I am very mechanically minded, and would expect to work closely with production in order to better understand the machinery, processes, timelines, expenses and demand levels.

  • "Collaborate with other event staff to specify the use of materials to produce work"

    This is something I would need more guidance with. I have had a great deal of material produced as part of various projects of my own, so I have some understanding of costings, requirements and solutions.

  • "Manage vendor relationships for outsource production"

    I am very familiar with performing this task- I've been doing this for over 15 years across industries and for for all kinds of vendors - ad agencies, printers, manufacturers, service providers, media buyers, warehousing & distribution, web production, bands, etc.

    This includes sourcing, hiring, managing and firing vendors.

  • "Oversee CAD building designs/construction drawings with internal staff and outsource partners"

    While I don't do CAD per-se, I am used to doing my own stand/event layouts and design in Illustrator and/or Photoshop. I'm a designer from way back and I understand the iterative process of design, as well as the communicative processes between designers, production and customers.

I strive for continuous improvement in all that I do.

PRINT PRODUCTION
  • "Provide strategic printing input"

    My experience here is client-side, but I have a great deal of it. Having seen the transition from 4 color process to digital, all kinds of tricks, coatings, materials and understanding a pretty good amount of costs/benefits/shortcomings of different processes & materials, I feel I could provide good input here.

  • "Develop and manage partnerships with production vendors"

    I am very familiar with performing this task- I've been doing this for over 15 years across industries and for for all kinds of vendors - ad agencies, printers, manufacturers, service providers, media buyers, warehousing & distribution, web production, bands, etc.

    This includes sourcing, hiring, managing and firing vendors.

  • "Oversee the overall quality of print production items"

    I always seek to exceed customer expectations, and would bring that experience to this task. I always ask for feedback and would make adjustments to processes as needed. I have managed print projects for over fifteen years

  • "Oversee work of on-site installations"

    I have performed this task long enough to know that something ALWAYS goes wrong, and one must be able to keep a cool head and find creative solutions on the fly.

    This is rarely done in a vacuum.

    I try to ally myself with solid, creative, intelligent teammates and vendors, who can be invaluable in finding swift solutions to unexpected challenges.

  • "Coordinate interactions of all event and production staff"

    I would make myself (or my proxy) the firewall between these two groups- you don't want every event staffer directing production staff or vice-versa.

    With that said, I would encourage positive collaboration and cooperation across these teams.

I love creating beauty from chaos.
