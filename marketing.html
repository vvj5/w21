MARKETING & TRADESHOW EVENTS
  • "Provide strategic event and tradeshow input"

    I have advised and organized all kinds of events - from small, local non-profit events to multi-day, multi-location, lavish corporate trade shows to massive, dirty, loud brewery and music events.

    I have made both cooperative and executive decisions for multiple organizations regarding:
    - Which shows to attend
    - What kind of events to produce
    - How much to budget & where to get the most bang for the buck.
    - How to go about planning for the events from the ground-up.

    I am good at thinking about tangential benefits/detriments and I am good at helping facilitate these decisions in pretty much any kind of environment.

  • "Conduct pre-event planning"

    I have done this in many different industries, styles, countries, sizes and budgets for nearly twenty years.

    This can include details and decisions such as:
    - Selection of which shows to attend (or which events to create)
    - Selection of booth space in venue(s)
    - Sourcing/negotiating/procuring venues/locations for events
    - Planning booth layout (or event map)
    - Working out the logistics of what to ship or buy on site
    - Thinking-through, budgeting and timelining for event rental/hiring/purchasing needs (toilets, trash, chairs, stage, lighting, sound system, security, bartenders, keg delivery, ice, serving glasses/plates/cutlery, hosts, cones, signage, negotiating with live bands, etc).

    I've conceived, planned, designed, sought vendors and sold spaces to "farmers market" type events

    I've conceived, planned, designed, bought gear, setup enormous events in difficult locations, recruited/scheduled and managed over 50 volunteers at once, made friends with local sheriffs and helped clean-up after thousands of people.

    I've made dozens of complex maps of venues, stand blueprints, staff assignment posts, parking areas, power points, distribution of all event facilities and people-flow.

    This is something I am very good at (and really enjoy), and I am happy to provide references (you can start with Brooklyn if you like ;) )

    Also see these examples:
    * 3 acre semi-outdoor brewery event site plan (4,000 attendees)
    * Multi-building/multi-outdoor space event site plan
    * Planning spreadsheet for brewery launch event

  • "Monitor deadlines"

    Deadlines are critical to successful event execution, and I always ensure that:

    - Show or venue deposit/reservations are made on time
    - Promotional ad space is bought and art is sent
    - Promotional activities are conducted correctly and at the correct time for the event (not too soon, not too late, generally more than one push of more than one method at more than one time)
    - Booth structure/media/merchandise/literature is designed, approved, produced and delivered on time
    - rental gear is reserved and shipping timing is verified (and checked on to make sure it got done)
    - Proprietary gear is shipped in time to arrive to correct site at the appointed time (and checked on to make sure it got done)
    - Accommodation is booked as necessary and in time and confirmation details are sent to all personnel as needed

    Also see these examples:
    * Work-In-Progress spreadsheet
    * Another Work-In-Progress spreadsheet

  • "Make assignments for the installation and dismantling of jobs"

    - I am usually able to plan this ahead of time or delegate teams to specific tasks.
    - I always put someone in charge of a team and liaise with them, not every team member.
    - If I am onsite, I will direct the setup/breakdown crew as necessary. This might involve roping-in salespeople.
    - If I am offsite, I will have an on-point person on the ground that I will keep a running dialog with to ensure all is running smoothly.
    - Ensure setup of show structures, optimally with the most critical pieces completed first (I would provide a list of priorities to setup crew)
    - Be sure the team has the instruction and materials and support they need to do their jobs.

    Also see these examples:
    * Organizing 50 volunteers

  • "Make and maintain event schedules"

    I am methodical and vigilant with these tasks, ensuring:

    - Booth staff (salespeople, usually) all have been sent multiple copies of the show runtimes and their shifts (if necessary). I usually have a list that I check off once a required verification of receipt has been received. I'll also usually slip a daily timeline sheet under their hotel room doors daily.
    - Band arrival, sound-check, runtimes, gear setup/takedown
    - The sound person to be onsite at the correct time to test gear, conduct sound-checks
    - bar servers stop serving by the required time
    - Stay on top of run-times, announcements, serving times, special event activity (surprises or whatever)

    Also see these examples:
    * Example show schedule

  • "Make and maintain construction schedules"

    - I would make sure all of the show gear has arrived at the correct place at the correct time, even if I am not there (I'd have a person responsible for receiving the gear, with instructions to call/text me when complete). I would call them if necessary.
    - Be sure the team has the instruction and materials and support they need to do their jobs.

  • "Monitor staff's on-site installation and dismantling of jobs"

    - I try to have a "go-to" team member onsite, whether I'm there or not. These people are usually the construction team's leader. I deal with them and keep my finger on the pulse of the construction/deconstruction progress.
    - If additional resources need to be allocated to make sure the timing of these activities is correct, I handle that too.
    - Be sure the team has what they need to do their jobs

  • "Conduct post-event evaluations"

    I always ensure that:

    - Staff/bands/vendors are paid in a timely manner
    - Thank you notes / follow-up questionnaires are sent in a timely manner
    - Leads gathered (in whatever form) are distributed to where they need to go.
